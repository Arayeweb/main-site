# Digital Atelier

A visual language for product micro-canvases — each frame a distilled artifact of what the software *does*, not what it is named.

Space is tight and intentional. Every preview lives inside a rounded rectangle that breathes like a viewport: generous internal padding, nothing touches the edge. Form communicates through layered translucency — frosted panels over saturated gradients, as if light passes through glass before hitting color.

Color is product-specific but family-coherent. Blues anchor web and trust; violet signals intelligence and comparison; cyan maps discovery and search; deep navy grounds identity and contact. Gradients move diagonally, never flat — the product feels alive, not stickered on.

Scale and rhythm follow a single rule: one hero element, two supporting details, one accent mark. The eye finds hierarchy in under two seconds. Icons and UI chrome are geometric, 1–2px strokes, rounded corners at a consistent radius — meticulously crafted to read at card scale.

Composition balances asymmetry with calm. Mock interfaces tilt slightly toward realism but stop before clutter — wireframe poetry, not screenshots. Each artifact should appear painstakingly composed, the product of deep expertise in interface illustration.

Text inside previews is absent or symbolic only — bars, dots, ranks — because meaning lives in spatial relationships. The viewer understands the product through visual grammar alone.

Master-level execution demands crisp edges, no overlapping elements, perfect alignment to an invisible grid. These are not decorations; they are miniature product portraits.
